# NYC-Flooding_CS439-Final-Project

Authors: Amber Safeer & Janis Lee

In July 2025, New York City witnessed two severe flash floods that cost human lives, and with the current climate crisis only worsening, events such as these are becoming more common. Our goal for this project is to identify whether the density of Green Infrastructure (rain gardens, infiltration basins, etc.) in an NYC neighborhood can accurately predict a reduction in maximum flood depths during high-intensity rain events, assessing both overall effectiveness of GIs and identifying high-risk regions for further GI implementation.
